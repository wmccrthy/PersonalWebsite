Course Description

An introduction to the ideas and techniques that allow computers to perform intelligently. The course will discuss methods of adversarial game playing and of solving general problems using heuristic search. It will also discuss the design of agents that learn either from experience or from a provided dataset. The course will cover both theoretical aspects of AI and practical considerations such as noisy sensor readings. Projects in the course are largely-based on the UC-Berkely course, CS 188, that uses a pacman framework for teaching AI concepts.
